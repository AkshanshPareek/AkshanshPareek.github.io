Akshansh Pareek

Hi, I am Akshansh Pareek, an emerging HCI/AI researcher and designer. I recently completed my Bachelor of Design at Delhi Technological University, where I trained in interaction design, UX research, and systems thinking while independently building technical skills in data science, machine learning, NLP, and computational social science.

I recently completed a research fellowship at Sentient Futures under the supervision of Julia Bettina Bossmann, where I researched LLM sycophancy and how emotional fine-tuning may entangle politeness, agreement, and factual reliability. My research focuses on Human-AI Interaction, Human-Agent Interaction, Responsible AI, NLP, dialogue systems, and participatory AI.

I design, build, and evaluate AI systems that are transparent, contestable, and socially grounded, especially in contexts of creativity, learning, trust, and community participation. Beyond research, I am a 3D artist, video game designer, and story writer for games. I worked on the indie game series Fears to Fathom, and outside work I enjoy 3D cinematography, yoga, ice skating, chess, and video games.
